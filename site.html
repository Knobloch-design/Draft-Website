     We have all seen that crytocurrency prices can swing wildly at the drop at the of a hat but is so new as an asset class that the vast majority of people don't understand how it works. Understanding what the factors that affect the price of crytocurrencies like bitcoin are all built apon a protical call the 'Block Chain'. Block Chain means that every transaction is recorded and publicly sharedy with everyone inside of any block, so that all transactions can be varified by the entire network. The network can be thought about as a random third party that has a computer. They use their computer to add you transaction to a block and then put said block at the top of the block chain. Every block will also contain a lock at the bottom who purpose is to ensure that it is more difficult for naer-do-wells not at false blocks onto the top of the block chain. Every lock has a key however, so the goal of every crytocurrency miner is to find that key before anyone else so they can claim a small portion of crytocurrency as a reward for their efforts. Here in lies the crux of my quesiton, which is how much do it actually cost to find the key to put a new block on to the block chain. The answer to this question is the theoretical bottom to the price of any crytocurrency as no miner is going to front the costs of all the electricly it costs to run their computer if it is just going to lose them money.

     The objective of this project was to identify ways to decrease medical insurance costs. Health insurance protects against unexpected medical emergency costs which, according to healthcare.gov, costs 9.12% of a worker's paycheck on average. Emergencies, pre-existing health conditions, lifestyle choices, and location could all have an effect on why the medical bills are high or low. Discovering what aspects increase medical insurance bills can help prevent or to plan for health insurance bills in the future. Our findings reveal attributes that could cost patients up to $20,000 more than average per year.
